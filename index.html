Fri · Jun 5

The Wedding 🤵👰

Suit and dress, all day. A close friend is getting married. Expect champagne, dancing, and Italians being Italians until very late.

Tip
Comfortable shoes you can dance in. Italian weddings are a marathon, not a sprint — they typically end around 2–3 AM. Pace the food: there will be a lot.

Week Two
South, and Back Up
Mon · Jun 8

Into the Langa, down to the sea

We pack and drive into the Langa — UNESCO hills, the heart of Barolo country. First stop La Morra for the panorama. Then lunch in the vineyards. After: we hunt down a butcher for a Fassona steak — four fingers thick, vacuum-packed for the road. Then down to Albenga on the Ligurian coast. Beach in the evening. And on the balcony tonight: that steak, on the grill. 🥩

  • morningDrive Torino → Langa (~1h30)

  • 13:30Lunch at Le Torri, Castiglione Falletto
  • pmButcher run: Fassona steak
  • late pmDrive Langa → Albenga (~2h30)
  • eveningBeach, then BBQ on the balcony 🔥
La Morra Belvedere
Highest village in the Barolo zone. The terrace looks over an ocean of vineyards.

Ristorante Le Torri Booked 13:30

Fassona Steak — Home Cookout
Ask the butcher to cut at least 4 fingers thick and vacuum-seal it. Let it rest out of the fridge for 1 hour before grilling. Coarse salt only — the meat does the rest.

Wed · Jun 10

Genova

We drive over, park, check in. The afternoon is ours to get lost in: Via Garibaldi and the Palazzi dei Rolli (UNESCO palazzos you can walk straight into), the San Lorenzo cathedral, the carruggi — the narrowest alleyway maze in Europe — and Boccadasse for sunset, a fishing village hidden inside the city.

A confession
Genova is the city. The one I keep telling you about — the one I think could be ours one day. Wear good shoes. I want to see what you think.
Via Garibaldi · Palazzi dei Rolli
A single street of 16th-century palaces, UNESCO-listed. Palazzo Rosso and Palazzo Bianco are open as museums.

Thu · Jun 11

Cinque Terre

Early-ish start. Drive to Levanto, check into the hotel, leave the car. Train to Riomaggiore. Via dell'Amore — booked. Then Manarola, then Vernazza — three of the five lands, each one steeper and prettier than the last. Night back in Levanto, by the sea.

  • amDrive Genova → Levanto (~1h30), check-in
  • trainLevanto → Riomaggiore
  • walkVia dell'Amore (30 min cliff path)
  • →Manarola — pastel houses on the cliff
  • →Vernazza — harbor, tower, swim if it's warm
  • eveningTrain back to Levanto, dinner by the sea
Via dell'Amore Booked
The famous coastal path between Riomaggiore and Manarola. Reopened in 2024 after 12 years of repairs. 30-min slots, max 200 people per slot, entrance from Riomaggiore.
🌐 viadellamore.info 📍 Map
Manarola
The most photographed of the five. The viewpoint from Punta Bonfiglio at sunset is the postcard shot.
📍 Map
Vernazza
Tiny natural harbor, Doria castle on the cliff. The path up to the castle is worth the climb.
📍 Map
Cinque Terre Card
Buy a Cinque Terre Treno MS Card at Levanto station (around €19/day). Covers unlimited trains between all five villages + the trail network. Via dell'Amore costs €10 extra on top.
Fri · Jun 12

Levanto → Parma → Milano

Morning drive to Parma. We walk the city: Piazza Duomo, the Battistero (pink Verona marble, one of the most beautiful in Italy), the Palazzo della Pilotta. Then — the day's main event — dinner at Antica Corte Pallavicina. A Michelin-starred restaurant inside a 14th-century castle on the Po river, where the Spigaroli brothers age culatello in the cellars beneath the dining room. After dinner, we drive on to Milan and check in.

  • amDrive Levanto → Parma (~2h30)
  • middayWalk Parma · Duomo, Battistero, Pilotta
  • pmDrive Parma → Polesine Parmense (~45 min)
  • eveningDinner at Antica Corte Pallavicina ⭐
  • nightDrive to Milano (~1h45), check-in
Battistero di Parma
12th-century octagonal baptistery in pink Verona marble. Outside and inside, one of the most beautiful religious buildings in Italy.
📍 Map 🌐 Tickets
Antica Corte Pallavicina ⭐ Booked
A 14th-century river castle on the Po, owned by the Spigaroli brothers. The cellars beneath your table age the most coveted culatello on earth. "Gastrofluvial" cuisine — fish from the river, herbs from the garden, recipes from another century.
📍 Map 🌐 Website ⭐ Michelin
Dress code reminder
Smart-elegant. Not black-tie, but Italian-elegant. A dress for you, a jacket for me. This is the dressiest meal of the trip.
